Marketing & Frontend

With the internet being the go-to source for fitness information, it can be daunting for someone looking to get fit to sift through all the available content and find information relevant to their physique. FitFind seeks to provide users with information on health and fitness, tailored to meet their unique fitness level and knowledge, helping people maintain their healthy lifestyles or start one.

Over the next year we have established several marketing goals that we feel will help FitFind grow. In order to get the word out we will initially target UW athletic clubs, since they seem to be the group most likely to be interested in a health and fitness application and spread the word out to friends. Once we have grown a small user base we will try and engage with them through Social Media. Our goal there will be to inform users of new or improved features in order to entice them to keep using FitFind. Additionally, we feel that having some sort of feedback mechanism will be helpful not only for initial troubleshooting with bugs that users have but also for tips that our users may have to improve FitFind.

Our users are those who want to find information about exercises, maintaining physical health, and nutrition. They can range from people who have not worked out in a long time and want to start and are looking for nearby gyms. They can also be someone who works daily and is looking for something to change up their routine with new exercises or a new running route. As well FitFind will be able to be a research source for elite athletes looking for specialized information on scientific studies or new supplements.

The competition we face will mostly will be similar search services like IDEA. IDEA is an integrative search engine for users to look for local fitness professionals, health clubs and fitness classes supported by a lot of sports partners. We will also face competition from sites like Health.com. While not a search service, they are still significant competitors due to the fact that they host the same content for which our service searches.

Potential collaborators we identified as being UW athlete clubs, local fitness centers and sports clubs which can provide us with information about their exercise events, types and levels. As well we feel that having FitFind embedded on websites of popular Youtube fitness gurus will be helpful since they tend to have millions of subscribers, and a simple plug would help spread the word out about FitFind.

We feel that our team will be able to accomplish these goals considering our skills sets. Our teams skill set varies widely, each member of the group brings something different. As a group, we have a good mix of front-end and back-end experience, with some having a creative eye for marketing and expansion. While others are knowledgeable about user needs and the nature of the content being dealt with.
